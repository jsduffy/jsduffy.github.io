What is FabEd?

FabEd, a collaboration between TIES and the Fab Foundation, is the only program providing students and communities with access to digital fabrication equipment AND a customized curricular framework to:

  • Enhance learning and encourage use of technology
  • Aligned with state standards
  • Support 21st century skills
  • Connect with the engineering design process
  • Stimulate creativity and artistic expression
  • Foster inquiry among students and teachers
  • Provide space for exploration and innovation
  • Engage students in real-world applications of STEM
  • Connect to other labs, schools, students, and ideas around the world
  • Prepare students for higher education and workforce needs
  • Aligned with ACT Work Keys/ONET as appropriate century skills

How does this site work?

The organization of this site is designed to mirror the project management processes used by the FabED initiative. There are additional sections of the site for resources and operational materials that apply to all projects.

Initiate
This area focuses on the work needed to “authorize” a project. Activities here include business development, supported by marketing and communications, as well as proposal development, negotiation, and a final contract.
Plan
This area focuses on planning and scheduling the work to perform the project. While it is useful to conduct some planning in the initiation phase, especially in support of a proposal, overall refinement is needed and formal project plan should be documented.
Execute
This area focuses on conducting the work itself, including project deliverables. It is through this area that work is improved and our new ‘gold standards’ are created for use on other projects. Communication of results to the team is critical.
Monitor
This area focuses on minoring and controlling the project by evaluating variances from the plan for cost, schedule and performance. It is within this area that corrective action happens.
Close
This area focuses on formal closing of a project contract and assuring acceptance by the client. Further, this is an appropriate time to step back and assess lessons learned and to capture key case studies.
